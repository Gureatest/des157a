journal post 2

date: october 23rd, 2023

As someone who uses a variety of services across several different platforms, nothing irks me more than a poor overlay. Whether it’s because it takes up to much space, distracts from the flow of using the app, or just has awful interactable objects such as closing buttons, poor overlays drastically reduce the enjoyment of a service.

The examples shown in the article are egregious, but it’s obvious that the authors sentiment does not come out of nowhere. I generally find overlays really annoying, especially in a time when web design is much easier to perform than before. Obviously, the article being from 2015 means that these types of websites are much rarer as people have learned the basics of web design. However, the points the author brings up can be applied to things beyond overlays..

Ideas such as how overlays should be user initiated can be applied to quite literally any element in web design. If you didn’t set it off, why is it there? If you do cause something to appear, is it visible to the user? Just asking simple questions when designing can avoid many of these flaws of web design.
